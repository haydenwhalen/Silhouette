For twenty years the structure that answered the question of who I was was the next race. Every meal, every workout, every sleep schedule, every relationship — they were all organized around whether they helped me make the next start line faster. When I retired, that structure dissolved overnight. And the disorientation that followed wasn't depression. It was the absence of the system that had been making my decisions for me since I was twelve. The hardest part of the transition wasn't the loss of competition. It was that there was no obvious next thing to be the best at, and the world doesn't wait while you figure that out. The temptation is to grab the first replacement structure that looks legitimate. The lesson, slowly, was that the in-between is the work — not an obstacle in the way of the work.
