The research finding that surprised me most is about moderate procrastinators. When we looked at how people generated original ideas, the people who delayed — not indefinitely, but for a measured period before committing — produced more creative output than the people who locked in their first viable answer. The slow-deciders weren't avoiding the work. They were incubating, and the additional time let unconventional possibilities surface that the fast-deciders never gave themselves the chance to see. There's an important asterisk: this finding doesn't license endless delay. It distinguishes spinning from incubating. The honest question for someone holding multiple paths is not 'why aren't you brave enough to choose?' It's 'are you using this time to let better options surface, and are you going to commit when that work is done?'
