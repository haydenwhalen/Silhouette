The rule I keep coming back to is: never miss twice. Missing once is an accident. Missing twice is the start of a new habit. The first time you skip the workout, that's life — you got sick, you traveled, the meeting ran late. The first miss doesn't break your trajectory. What breaks the trajectory is the spiral of shame after the first miss, where you decide that because today was off, this week is shot, this month is shot, the whole effort is shot. That's the actual collapse — and it's never the lapse itself, it's the second day. So the rule grants permission to miss any single session, provided you return the next day. It gives you a concrete, low-stakes definition of 'still in,' and it removes the shame that has been doing more damage than the lapse was ever going to.
