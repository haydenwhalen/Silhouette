What I want you to know, before anything else, is that the flatness you're describing — the work that used to matter not mattering, the feelings that used to be there being muted, the sense of having lost something inside yourself — that's not a character defect. It's not evidence that you were never really invested. It's not proof that you've gotten lazy or that you've lost your edge. The data on how human emotional systems work tell a different story. When the cost of feeling at full volume becomes unsustainable — because of accumulated stress, because of grief, because of carrying things you didn't have space to process — the system regulates. The signal gets quieter. Things stop landing. That's not failure. That's protection. The first move isn't to push harder against the flatness. The first move is to recognize that the flatness is information about what's been happening to you, and to give yourself permission to take it as that — not as a verdict on who you are.
