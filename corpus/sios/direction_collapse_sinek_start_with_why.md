Let me give you the pattern I kept noticing. Every inspiring leader and organization, regardless of size or field, thinks, acts, and communicates in the exact same way — and it is the complete opposite of everyone else. I call it the Golden Circle. Most people know what they do. Some of them know how they do it. But very few can clearly say why they do it — and by why I don't mean to make money; that's a result. I mean your purpose, your cause, the reason you get out of bed in the morning. Here's the thing: we normally communicate from the outside in, from the clearest thing to the fuzziest — what, then how, then why. But the people who actually inspire go from the inside out: why, then how, then what. So if you feel lost about your own direction, notice where you keep starting. You're almost certainly starting with the what — what job, what title, what next step. Try starting with the why. Get the belief underneath it clear, and the what stops feeling so arbitrary. Because people don't buy what you do; they buy why you do it. And that includes the story you've been selling yourself about your own life.
