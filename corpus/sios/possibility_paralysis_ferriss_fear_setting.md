content_summary: "In his April 2017 TED talk, Tim Ferriss presents \"fear-setting\" — a Stoic-derived exercise he describes as the most valuable exercise he does every month. The core move: instead of goal-setting, define your fears in writing. For each feared option, write out the worst-case scenario in detail, what you could do to prevent or reduce it, and what you could do to repair the damage if it happened. Then estimate both the likely upside and the cost of inaction — \"what will it cost you in six months, twelve months, three years if you don't take action?\" Ferriss' key observation for the paralysis user: what we fear doing is very often exactly what we need to do, and most feared setbacks are temporary and more recoverable than imagined. Most people will choose unhappiness over uncertainty, and the cost of that choice compounds over time."
