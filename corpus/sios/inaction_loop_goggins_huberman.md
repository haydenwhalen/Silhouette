People want to talk about motivation. They want to talk about discipline. They want the secret. But here's what nobody wants to hear: most people are not stuck. They've made a decision. It's a quiet decision — you don't even notice when you made it — but at some point, you said yes to a version of yourself that doesn't do the hard thing. That doesn't have the conversation. That doesn't start. That knows what they need to do and finds a thousand reasons every day not to do it. That's not a circumstances problem. That's not a timing problem. That's not a motivation problem. That is who you have decided to be. And until you look at that directly — until you're honest that you are the one who keeps saying yes to this version of yourself — nothing changes. You can get the best planner, the best coach, the best system. And you will still find a way not to use them. Because the problem isn't the plan. The problem is the person who made the plan has already decided they're not going to follow through.
