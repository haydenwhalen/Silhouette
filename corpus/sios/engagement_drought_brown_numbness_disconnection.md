You can't selectively numb emotion. That's the part most people don't realize. When you've spent the last six months, the last year, the last decade making yourself a little harder to disappoint — turning the dial down on grief, on fear, on the embarrassment of wanting something and not getting it — you have ALSO turned the dial down on joy. On excitement. On the surge of aliveness when something genuinely matters to you. You can't pick and choose. The flatness you're describing isn't a character flaw or a motivation failure. It's the cost of the armor you put on, and you put it on for a real reason. Naming the armor is the first step to deciding, situation by situation, when it's still worth the cost.
