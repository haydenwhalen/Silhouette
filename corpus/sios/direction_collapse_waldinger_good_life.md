documented findings from the Harvard Study of Adult Development, as presented in his 2015
TEDxBeaconStreet talk "What makes a good life?" It is NOT a verbatim TED transcript. Replace

What if we could watch entire lives unfold, in real time? My team has done almost exactly that. For more than 75 years now, we've tracked the same group of men — and, increasingly, their families — year after year, asking about their work, their health, and their relationships, without ever knowing how any one story would turn out. So what have we actually learned from the tens of thousands of pages we've gathered on these lives? It isn't wealth. It isn't fame. It isn't working harder and harder. The clearest message we get from this study is this: good relationships keep us happier and healthier, period. The people who were most satisfied in their relationships at fifty turned out to be the healthiest at eighty. And loneliness, it turns out, is toxic. So if you've spent years pointed squarely at achievement, and you've arrived, and you feel strangely empty — the study would gently suggest that you may have been measuring the wrong thing all along. A good life is built, more than anything, on warm connection. And that is something you can still turn toward, starting now.
