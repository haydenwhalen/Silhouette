Happiness and joy aren't the same thing. Happiness is conditional — it's tied to an outcome. The deal closes, the kid behaves, the workout goes the way you wanted, the role lands. When the conditions are met, you get happy. When they're not, you don't. That's fine. But joy isn't sitting on top of conditions like that. Joy is something that runs underneath. When you've spent ten years optimizing for the outcome and you arrive at it and you don't feel anything — the lesson is not that you chose the wrong outcome. The lesson is that you've been hunting in the category that pays out in happiness moments and never in joy. Joy doesn't show up where you've been looking, because where you've been looking is for conditions to be met. The shift is not a new target. It's a different question.
