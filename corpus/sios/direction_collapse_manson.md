There's a story embedded in modern self-improvement culture that goes something like this: everyone has a singular passion or calling, and the path to a good life is to find it and pursue it. The corollary is that if you haven't found it, something is wrong with you — you're not looking hard enough, you're settling, you're in denial. This story is wrong. For most people, there is no singular calling waiting to be discovered. Most lives are made of multiple interests, shifting priorities, work that becomes meaningful through the doing rather than work that was meaningful before you started. The crisis you're in is not that you've failed to find your passion. The crisis is that you've internalized a cultural script that told you having one was the standard, and now you're judging yourself against a fictional benchmark. The way out isn't to search harder for the missing calling. It's to notice that the question itself was malformed.
