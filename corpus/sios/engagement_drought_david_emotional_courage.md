Here is something we get wrong about feeling stuck and flat: we assume the fix is to be more
positive. So we push the difficult feeling down, or we put a bright face on it, and we call that
coping. But bottling what you actually feel, or brooding on it while performing positivity on the
outside, is precisely how the things that used to matter go quiet. The discomfort you're trying to
manage away is not evidence that something is wrong with you. Difficult emotions are normal — they
are data, not a defect — and discomfort is, in a real sense, the price of admission to a meaningful
life. You don't re-engage by feeling better on command. You re-engage by letting the real feeling
be present and listening to what it's telling you about what you care about. The aliveness comes
back on the other side of being honest, not on the other side of being relentlessly upbeat.
