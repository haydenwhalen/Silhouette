The reason restarting a dropped habit feels so hard isn't a character failing — it's limbic friction: the activation energy your nervous system requires to override its current state and initiate a behavior that's lost its automaticity. You can reduce that friction by timing the restart for the high-norepinephrine window (first 0–8 hours after waking), visualizing the procedural steps beforehand, and — critically — not compensating with double effort. Just get back on the horse the next day.
