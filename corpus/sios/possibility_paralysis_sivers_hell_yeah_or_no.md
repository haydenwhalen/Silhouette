content_summary: "On Tim Ferriss Show episode #125 (December 2015), Derek Sivers explains the \"Hell Yeah or No\" filter — a decision rule he developed when his calendar was full of obligations he had said yes to out of politeness or mild interest. The concept: if you are asked to do something and you're not feeling \"fuck yeah, that's amazing,\" say no. The reason is that most people say yes to too many mediocre things, and those mediocre yeses fill up all the time and energy that would otherwise be available for the rare thing that genuinely merits a \"hell yeah.\" The direct implication for someone with a list of options they are pursuing half-heartedly: the problem is not which option to choose — it's that none of them has cleared the hell-yeah bar, and the solution is to stop treating mild interest as a reason to act. Sivers later published a book of the same name (Hell Yeah or No, 2020) elaborating the principle."
