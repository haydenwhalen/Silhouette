⚠️ RECONSTRUCTION NOTE: The text below is a faithful paraphrase of Tim Urban's documented
argument from his 2016 TED talk "Inside the mind of a master procrastinator." It is NOT a

So here's what I figured out about my own brain. There's a Rational Decision-Maker in there — the part that knows the paper is due and the gym is a good idea. But there's also this other character, the Instant Gratification Monkey, and the monkey has exactly one principle: easy and fun, right now. The moment the work gets hard, the monkey grabs the wheel and steers us into the Dark Playground — all those hours of refreshing the same pages and reorganizing the desk that aren't even fun, because the whole time you know you should be doing something else. The only thing the monkey is afraid of is the Panic Monster, who stays asleep until a deadline gets close enough — and then, suddenly, you can work. Here's the part that actually keeps me up at night. The Panic Monster only wakes up when there's a deadline. So for the big things in life that have no deadline — the career you keep meaning to build, the person you keep meaning to call — the monkey just wins, quietly, for years. That kind of procrastination doesn't feel like an emergency. That's exactly why it's so dangerous.
