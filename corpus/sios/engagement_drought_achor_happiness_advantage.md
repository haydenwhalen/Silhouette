⚠️ RECONSTRUCTION NOTE: The text below is a faithful paraphrase of Shawn Achor's documented
argument from his 2011 TEDxBloomington talk "The happy secret to better work." It is NOT a

Most of us have the formula for success and happiness completely backwards. We believe it goes like this: if I work harder, I'll be more successful, and if I'm more successful, then I'll be happy. The trouble is, every single time your brain has a success, you just move the goalpost of what success looks like. You hit your target — and the target immediately slides further out. So happiness keeps slipping over the cognitive horizon, and your brain never actually gets to arrive. Now here's what the research on positive psychology actually shows. If we can raise a person's level of positivity in the present, their brain experiences what we call a happiness advantage: your brain at positive performs significantly better than it does at negative, neutral, or stressed. Intelligence rises. Creativity rises. Energy rises. Nearly every business and educational outcome we measure improves. So the point is this: happiness is not the reward you finally collect after you've forced yourself to engage with your work. It's the fuel that lets you engage with it at all. Which means you don't have to wait for the work to feel good before you can show up for it well. You can reverse the whole formula.
