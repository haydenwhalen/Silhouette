Disorientation after a major transition is not evidence that you've lost yourself — it's evidence that your sense of self was organized around something that's now gone. The thing you lost wasn't you. It was a structure your identity lived inside. Grant's reframe: anchor identity to what you value, not what role you play, and the disorientation of transition becomes navigable — because values survive the transition even when the structure doesn't.
