I used to think the whole point of life was to pursue happiness. But after years of studying this, I came to a different conclusion. Our culture is obsessed with happiness — and yet, in chasing it, a lot of people end up feeling emptier, because happiness is a state that comes and goes. What actually sustains people is meaning. And unlike happiness, meaning is something you build. I found that it rests on four pillars. The first is belonging — being in relationships where you are valued for who you intrinsically are, and where you value others too. The second is purpose — having something to give, not just something to get; a reason to get up in the morning. The third is transcendence — those rare moments when you're lifted above the rush and the noise of daily life, and your sense of self fades. And the fourth is storytelling — the narrative you tell yourself about your own life, which you actually have the power to edit and revise. So if you feel adrift, the more useful question may not be "what will make me happy?" It might be: where do I belong, what can I contribute, and what story am I telling about who I am and how I got here?
