Resistance from his book "The War of Art" (2002) and his 2015 Tim Ferriss interview, in his

When most people talk about why they're not doing the work they know they should be doing, they reach for the usual vocabulary — procrastination, laziness, fear, self-sabotage. None of those words are accurate. They're explanations after the fact. The honest name for what you're experiencing is Resistance. Resistance is a force. It has its own intelligence, and its intelligence is aimed with extraordinary precision at exactly the thing you most need to do. That's how you know what your real work is, by the way — Resistance points to it. The more important the work, the louder the Resistance. So when you find yourself in that loop — knowing what you should do, planning to do it, then not doing it, then judging yourself for not doing it — the first move is to stop reading that loop as evidence of a character defect. It's not. It's evidence that Resistance is functioning exactly as Resistance functions. Naming it is the first move. Once you can see it as a force outside of you that is doing its job, you stop fighting yourself and start dealing with the actual obstacle.
