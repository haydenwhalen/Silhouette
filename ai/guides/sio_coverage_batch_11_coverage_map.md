## Per-state coverage + white-space

### direction-collapse (33) — type story:12 reframe:9 mechanism:7 permission:5
**Well covered (saturated):** elite-athlete / celebrity "I reached the peak and it felt empty" (Tom Brady,
Jonny Wilkinson, Jordan Burroughs, Katelyn Ohashi, Steven Bartlett, Rainn Wilson, McConaughey×2);
dopamine/happiness science (Huberman×2, Dan Gilbert, Lieberman, Tal Ben-Shahar, Robert Waldinger);
"follow your passion is bad" (Newport, Manson); "purpose develops slowly / it's ok not to know"
(Damon, Adichie, Mo Rocca, Greene, Federer, Toby Hendy).
**Thin / white-space:**
- The **ordinary young professional** who "did everything right" — good job, checked the boxes — and quietly lost the plot, with **no dramatic peak** to point at.
- Disillusionment with a **cause / field / mission** you believed in (not personal-achievement emptiness).
- **Grief / loss** as the trigger for directionlessness.
- **Money specifically** for normal earners (vs celebrities turning down $14.5M).
- Non-US / non-celebrity lived voices.

### engagement-drought (32) — type reframe:9 permission:8 mechanism:8 story:7
**Well covered:** languishing-naming (Adam Grant, Corey Keyes); dopamine/movement science (Huberman×2,
Wendy Suzuki, McGonigal, Sapolsky); numbing (Brené Brown×2); comfort-crisis (Michael Easter); hedonic
treadmill (Achor, Gawdat, Robbins); mastery/S-curve boredom (Whitney Johnson, Buckingham, Arthur Brooks).
**Thin / white-space:**
- **Awe / wonder / novelty** as the antidote to flatness — Dacher Keltner's awe research is absent.
- **Relational / community** flatness (going-through-motions with people, not just work).
- The quiet **joy of craft / depth** — re-engaging through getting good at something hard.
- **Generative boredom** (boredom as a doorway, not a defect).
- Rest/sabbatical as legitimate (only Arianna Huffington touches it).

### identity-transition (31) — type reframe:11 story:10 mechanism:5 permission:5
**Well covered:** elite-athlete/performer retirement (Wambach, Agassi, Ohno, Tony Hawk, Vonn, Shaun White,
Jack Green); liminality-naming (Ibarra, Bruce Feiler, Sarah Thomas, Pauline Boss); neuroplasticity-self
(Huberman, Eagleman, Lara Boyd, Barrett); leaving-a-world (Megan Phelps-Roper, Maria Shriver).
**Thin / white-space:**
- **Ordinary career rupture** — laid off, profession left, company failed — the non-athlete version.
- **New parenthood** as identity overwrite.
- **Immigration / cultural / language** identity transition.
- The **excited, generative** side of reinvention (most entries frame transition as loss).
- Mid-life or **forced** reinvention via health.

### inaction-loop (33) — type reframe:12 mechanism:8 permission:7 story:6  (richest state)
**Well covered:** procrastination science (Pychyl, Tim Urban, Pressfield/Resistance, Thomas Curran,
Burkeman); motivation-is-unreliable (Jocko, Mel Robbins 5-sec, Tony Robbins); fear-dissolves-by-moving
(Ryan Holiday, McGonigal); perfectionism (Saujani, Gilbert, Curran); identity-based action (Goggins,
Mylett, Stulberg, Clear).
**Thin / white-space:**
- **Overwhelm-freeze** — too much on the plate → shutdown (distinct from fear or laziness).
- **Task-initiation / executive function**, framed non-clinically (the "I literally can't start" wall).
- **Self-forgiveness after procrastinating** as the thing that unsticks the next action (Pychyl's own research).
- "The task itself feels **pointless / meaningless**" (not fear, not perfectionism).
- **Environment / friction design** beyond Mel/Tony's "negotiation."

### momentum-gap (29) — type reframe:8 permission:8 story:7 mechanism:6
**Well covered:** social-comparison / "behind my peers" (Laurie Santos, Morgan Housel, Sahil Bloom,
Simon Sinek, Mel Robbins, Sara Blakely); restart-friction science (Huberman limbic friction, BJ Fogg,
Rich Diviney, Katy Milkman); self-compassion-after-lapse (Kristin Neff, Clear×2, Jesse Itzler);
timeline-is-your-own (Barbara Kasten, Joyce DiDonato, Allyson Felix).
**Thin / white-space:**
- **Re-entry after burnout / illness / a long forced break** (vs the comparison framing that dominates).
- **Energy-depletion** momentum loss (you're not behind; you're empty).
- The **first small win / proof to yourself** as the restart mechanism.
- The **long unglamorous grind** / consistency compounding (only Andy Frisella).
- Restarting after a **public failure**, framed as ordinary (Scott Galloway touches it).

### possibility-paralysis (29) — type reframe:10 mechanism:8 permission:6 story:5
**Well covered:** choice-overload science (Schwartz, Iyengar, McGinnis/FOBO, Ruth Chang parity);
decision frameworks (Bezos regret-min, Sivers hell-yeah, McKeown essentialism, Ferriss fear-setting);
commitment-as-freedom (Pete Davis, David Whyte); act-into-clarity (Ibarra); start-from-what-you-don't-want
(Kutcher).
**Thin / white-space:**
- **Tiny reversible bets / small experiments** instead of one big irreversible call.
- **Values / identity-based** deciding ("which option is most *me*").
- **Decision fatigue** — too many small choices draining the capacity to make the big one.
- **Trusting intuition / the body** in a decision (vs more analysis).
- Fear of **closing doors** reframed as the cost of openness itself.

## How this map is used

`sio_coverage_batch_11_targets.md` converts each white-space line above into concrete target slots
(11 per state). Every new SIO must cite which slot it fills and which existing SIOs it must not duplicate.
Sourcing is caption-verified (yt-dlp) — candidate directions in the targets doc are **unverified leads**
until a real transcript + timestamp is confirmed.
