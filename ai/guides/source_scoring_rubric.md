# Silhouette — Source Scoring Rubric

> **How to use this rubric:** Use this document to evaluate any potential source before adding it to the Silhouette corpus. Score each dimension from 1–5, apply the weights, calculate the weighted total, and apply the critical threshold rules. A source must pass both the weighted score threshold AND the critical dimension minimums to be considered for inclusion.
>
> This rubric produces a **source-level assessment** — whether a source is worth investing in at all. After a source passes source-level evaluation, individual episodes still require separate content-level review before ingestion.

---

## The 8 Evaluation Dimensions

### Dimension 1: Stuck-State Relevance
**Weight: 3×**

Does this source directly address the stuck states in the User Problem Model — particularly the three MVP priority states: Direction Collapse, Engagement Drought, and Inaction Loop?

"Topically adjacent" is not relevance. A podcast about entrepreneurship is not automatically relevant to Inaction Loop. A specific episode where a credible founder describes the identity shift required before they could start something is relevant. Judge at the content level, not the topic level.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Directly addresses 2+ MVP states with high specificity and personal depth. Content is core Silhouette material. |
| 4 | Directly addresses at least 1 MVP state with high specificity, plus touches a second state (MVP or non-MVP). |
| 3 | Addresses 1 MVP state with moderate specificity, or addresses 1–2 non-MVP states well. Requires selective episode curation. |
| 2 | Occasionally relevant to MVP states but primarily covers adjacent territory. Very selective ingestion required. |
| 1 | Does not meaningfully address any defined stuck state. Not eligible. |

**Critical minimum: Must score ≥3. Below 3 → automatic exclusion regardless of total score.**

---

### Dimension 2: Insight Density
**Weight: 3×**

What is the ratio of genuinely retrievable, state-relevant moments to total content volume in this source's best episodes?

Evaluate against the source's best material — not the average episode. We will curate episodes selectively, so the question is: when this source is at its best, how dense is it?

A retrievable insight must: be attributable to a specific person; address a defined stuck state; offer a reframe, mechanism, or story that goes beyond generic advice; and stand alone without requiring full episode context.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | ≥5 retrievable insights per hour in best episodes. Consistently high across the source's strongest material. |
| 4 | 3–5 retrievable insights per hour in best episodes. Quality is consistent in the source's stronger material. |
| 3 | 1–3 retrievable insights per hour in best episodes. Requires careful selection but worth the effort. |
| 2 | Fewer than 1 retrievable insight per hour on average, even in best episodes. Very low yield. |
| 1 | Essentially no retrievable insights — generic, surface, or non-attributable content throughout. |

**Critical minimum: Must score ≥3. Below 3 → automatic exclusion regardless of total score.**

---

### Dimension 3: Speaker Credibility
**Weight: 2×**

Would a young professional (22–32) in the target demographic find this speaker credible, trustworthy, and worth listening to? Credibility comes from relevant lived experience, documented expertise, or track record — not follower count or mainstream fame.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Speaker has direct, lived experience in the domains Silhouette serves AND is widely respected by the target demographic. Credibility is specific and strong. |
| 4 | Strong credibility in a relevant domain. Target demographic would find them trustworthy. Minor gaps compensated by track record or expertise. |
| 3 | Adequate credibility — the target demographic would not question this speaker but might not seek them out specifically. |
| 2 | Limited or domain-mismatched credibility. Some users would trust them; others would dismiss them. |
| 1 | Credibility is low, questionable, or primarily based on social media presence. Target demographic would be skeptical. |

**Critical minimum: Must score ≥3. Below 3 → automatic exclusion regardless of total score.**

---

### Dimension 4: Emotional Resonance
**Weight: 2×**

Does this source produce content that lands emotionally — not just intellectually? Does it name experiences in a way that makes users feel accurately seen, rather than just informed?

Emotional resonance is what makes an insight feel like "this person is talking about me" rather than "that's an interesting point."

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Consistently produces the "naming the exact experience" quality. Content lands at the emotional level before the intellectual level. High likelihood of aha moments. |
| 4 | Often produces emotional resonance, especially in personal disclosure or narrative segments. Not consistent throughout but present and meaningful. |
| 3 | Some emotional resonance, primarily in personal story segments. More intellectual than emotional overall. |
| 2 | Primarily intellectual or instructional. Rarely produces the emotional naming quality. |
| 1 | No emotional resonance — purely informational, lecture-style, or motivational without personal depth. |

---

### Dimension 5: Specificity of Experience
**Weight: 2×**

Does the speaker speak from their own specific, personal experience — naming particular moments, decisions, and realizations — or do they speak generically about what people in these situations should do?

The insight that lands is "I remember the exact moment I realized I had been running from something" — not "many people find that reflection is useful."

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Speaker consistently speaks from direct, specific personal experience. Moments are named with precision — particular years, decisions, feelings, turning points. |
| 4 | Often speaks from personal experience with specificity. Some sections are more general but anchored by personal narrative. |
| 3 | Mix of personal experience and general framing. Personal moments present but not dominant. |
| 2 | Primarily speaks about experiences in the third person ("people often feel...") with occasional personal reference. |
| 1 | Entirely generic. No personal experience shared. Advisory mode throughout. |

---

### Dimension 6: Content Extractability
**Weight: 2×**

Can individual moments from this source be extracted and understood without the surrounding context? A retrievable insight must convey its full meaning when encountered in isolation — without requiring the user to have heard the full episode. This dimension assesses the *content* — whether the insight itself can stand alone.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Consistently produces moments that stand alone perfectly. Clear, well-formed statements. Insights don't require setup to land. |
| 4 | Most meaningful moments are reasonably self-contained. Minimal context needed in the corpus metadata. |
| 3 | Meaningful moments exist but are often embedded in long exchanges. Can be extracted with effort and some framing added. |
| 2 | Most meaningful content is deeply embedded. Extraction requires significant reformatting — increasing risk of drift from the original. |
| 1 | Content is not retrievable in discrete chunks. Must be experienced as a whole. |

---

### Dimension 7: Transcript Availability
**Weight: 1×**

Is clean, accurate text available for this source with manageable effort? This dimension assesses the *format* — whether a usable text layer exists, independent of whether the content itself extracts well (see Dimension 6).

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Official transcripts available. Clean, accurate, timestamped. |
| 4 | Auto-generated transcripts of good quality (Spotify, YouTube), or unofficial transcripts of reasonable quality. Timestamps available. |
| 3 | Auto-captions available but require editing. Can be cleaned at manageable cost. |
| 2 | No transcripts. Audio-only. Transcription possible but adds significant cost per episode. |
| 1 | Format not extractable (purely visual, no text layer, protected format). |

---

### Dimension 8: Temporal Stability
**Weight: 1×**

Does the content remain useful and accurate over time? Timeless content stays relevant for 3+ years. Time-sensitive content dates quickly due to cultural references, current events, or evolving claims.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Essentially timeless. Insights about human experience, identity, and motivation do not date. |
| 4 | Stable with minor exceptions. Some cultural references may age, but core insights are durable. |
| 3 | Moderate shelf life. Some specific recommendations or references may date, but most insights remain relevant. |
| 2 | Meaningfully time-sensitive. Significant portions may feel dated within 1–2 years. |
| 1 | Primarily time-sensitive. Not suitable for a durable corpus. |

## Critical Threshold Rules

Apply these rules before calculating the total. Failure on any critical dimension is an automatic exclusion.

**Rule 1:** Stuck-State Relevance < 3 → **automatic exclusion.**
**Rule 2:** Insight Density < 3 → **automatic exclusion.**
**Rule 3:** Speaker Credibility < 3 → **automatic exclusion.**

A source that fails any one of these three dimensions is not eligible for the Silhouette corpus, regardless of its total weighted score.

---

## Score Interpretation and Tier Thresholds

| Weighted Score | Tier | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 65–80 | **MVP Priority** | Proceed to episode-level evaluation immediately. High priority for first corpus version. |
| 50–64 | **Strong Candidate** | Include in second iteration, or for specific state gaps not covered by MVP Priority sources. Evaluate specific episodes before committing. |
| 35–49 | **Conditional** | Only if a critical gap exists in that state that cannot be filled by higher-scoring sources. Document the reason for inclusion explicitly. |
| Below 35 | **Exclude** | Do not include. Document the decision and reasoning. |

**Note on strong candidates with high state-specific fit:** A source scoring 55–64 with a very strong state-specific score (e.g., excellent for Inaction Loop specifically) should be included for that state even if it doesn't qualify as MVP Priority overall. The tier system is a guideline; document the rationale when deviating.

---

## Source-Level vs. Episode-Level Evaluation

This rubric evaluates a **source** as a whole. It determines whether a source is worth investing in at all, and is calibrated against the source's best episodes for density-related dimensions.

After a source passes source-level evaluation, individual episodes require separate content-level review:
- Is this episode relevant to a defined stuck state?
- What is the primary state tag for this episode's best segments?
- What segments within this episode are high enough density to ingest?
- Does the guest in this episode have the right credibility for the content they're delivering?

Episode-level evaluation belongs to the Corpus / Ingestion Pipeline component, not Source Strategy.
