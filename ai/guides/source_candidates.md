# Silhouette — Source Candidates

> **How to use this document:** This is the working list of evaluated and pending source candidates for the Silhouette corpus. Use it alongside `source_scoring_rubric.md` to track evaluation decisions. Update candidate status as formal evaluations are completed. Before ingesting any source, that source must appear here with a documented decision.
>
> **Important:** Preliminary scores and assessments in this document are based on general knowledge of these sources. They are starting points for formal evaluation — not final decisions. Actual episode-level review is required before any source is cleared for ingestion.

---

## Status Definitions

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **MVP Priority** | Scored 65–80 on rubric; cleared for episode-level evaluation immediately |
| **Strong Candidate** | Scored 50–64; include in second iteration or for specific state gaps |
| **Pending Evaluation** | Has not yet been formally scored against the rubric |
| **Deprioritized** | Evaluated; not excluded but not worth pursuing for the MVP |
| **Excluded** | Formally rejected; do not revisit without new information |

## Section 1: Primary Source Candidates

### 1.1 Long-Form Interview Podcasts

---

**Tim Ferriss Show**
- *Type:* Long-form interview podcast
- *Host:* Tim Ferriss
- *Preliminary score: 69 / 80 — MVP Priority*

What makes it strong: Tim Ferriss is exceptionally skilled at eliciting specific, personal moments from his guests — the exact turning points, realizations, and identity shifts that produce retrievable insights. His guest list is among the most credible available (world-class performers, thinkers, and practitioners across domains). Ferriss himself has high credibility with the target demographic as a practitioner who has navigated career direction collapse and reinvention firsthand. Episodes frequently produce standalone quotable moments.

Strongest states: Direction Collapse (career clarity and reinvention guests), Inaction Loop (execution, resistance, starting), Possibility Paralysis (commitment and decision guests)

Weaknesses: Episodes run very long (2–3 hours); density varies significantly by guest. Requires careful episode selection. Some episodes are domain-specific (investing, military) with lower direct relevance.

Episode curation guidance: Prioritize episodes where the guest explicitly discusses their own stuck state and what shifted — not episodes focused on tactical skills or domain-specific expertise. Naval Ravikant, Ryan Holiday, Matthew McConaughey, Cal Newport, and David Goggins appearances are likely the highest-density for Silhouette.

*Status: MVP Priority — begin episode-level evaluation*

---

**Diary of a CEO (Steven Bartlett)**
- *Type:* Long-form interview podcast / YouTube
- *Host:* Steven Bartlett
- *Preliminary score: 69 / 80 — MVP Priority*

What makes it strong: Diary of a CEO is the most directly aligned podcast with Silhouette's target demographic of any source in this list. Steven Bartlett explicitly targets young professionals navigating career, purpose, and life direction. He is both credible (founded and sold a major company in his mid-20s) and skilled at drawing personal vulnerability from guests. The show has a strong track record of producing emotionally resonant moments on Direction Collapse and Inaction Loop.

Strongest states: Direction Collapse, Inaction Loop, Engagement Drought

Weaknesses: Steven Bartlett's interview style is occasionally leading. Some guest selections are more celebrity-facing than insight-dense. Back catalog quality has improved significantly in recent years — more recent episodes tend to be stronger.

Episode curation guidance: Prioritize guests who have navigated a visible stuck state (career change, reinvention, overcoming inaction). Solo episodes by Bartlett reflecting on his own experience are also high-density. Avoid celebrity interview episodes without a clear stuck-state narrative.

*Status: MVP Priority — begin episode-level evaluation*

---

**School of Greatness (Lewis Howes)**
- *Type:* Long-form interview podcast
- *Host:* Lewis Howes
- *Preliminary score: 62 / 80 — Strong Candidate*

What makes it strong: School of Greatness has one of the deepest back catalogs of any podcast in this space, with guests who have specifically navigated Direction Collapse and Identity Transition. Lewis Howes has personal credibility as someone who rebuilt his life after a serious injury ended his athletic career. Emotional resonance is the show's strongest dimension — guests frequently open up at a depth unusual for this format.

Strongest states: Direction Collapse, Identity Transition, Engagement Drought

Weaknesses: Insight density is variable across episodes — some are excellent, many are moderate. Lewis Howes's interview technique produces emotional resonance but occasionally at the expense of specificity. Guest selection quality varies. Format retrievability is inconsistent (transcript availability varies by episode).

Episode curation guidance: Curate heavily. Focus on guests with a clear reinvention arc or direction-finding story. Avoid episodes where the guest's content is primarily motivational without personal depth. Key guests to prioritize: Matthew McConaughey, Jay Shetty, Brené Brown appearances, Rich Roll.

*Status: Strong Candidate — prioritize high-signal back catalog episodes*

---

**The Knowledge Project (Shane Parrish / Farnam Street)**
- *Type:* Long-form interview podcast
- *Host:* Shane Parrish
- *Preliminary score: 64 / 80 — Strong Candidate (near MVP threshold)*

What makes it strong: Shane Parrish is one of the best interviewers available for eliciting deep, specific thinking from his guests. The Knowledge Project consistently produces the highest-quality intellectual insights of any interview show — clean, well-formed, standalone quotable moments. Excellent for Possibility Paralysis (decision-making, mental models for choosing) and Direction Collapse (what direction actually is, how people find it).

Strongest states: Possibility Paralysis, Direction Collapse

Weaknesses: More intellectual than emotional — emotional resonance is the show's lowest dimension. The target demographic tends toward growth-oriented professionals, which is a good match, but the tone is more measured and analytical than visceral. Transcript access varies (some behind Farnam Street paywall).

Episode curation guidance: Best episodes are with guests who bring both intellectual depth and personal narrative — the most relevant guests include Naval Ravikant, Cal Newport, Adam Grant, Annie Duke. Avoid guests whose content is primarily domain-specific without personal experience of stuck states.

*Status: Strong Candidate — high priority for Possibility Paralysis and intellectually-oriented Direction Collapse content*

---

**How I Built This (Guy Raz)**
- *Type:* Long-form interview podcast (NPR)
- *Host:* Guy Raz
- *Preliminary score: 59 / 80 — Strong Candidate*

What makes it strong: How I Built This is the best available source for Inaction Loop content — founders describing the specific, personal experience of starting something despite fear, uncertainty, and every reason not to. The narrative format produces exceptional specificity of experience. NPR transcript quality is excellent.

Strongest states: Inaction Loop (the start-despite-fear narrative), Possibility Paralysis (commitment decisions)

Weaknesses: Stuck-state relevance is narrower than other sources — the show is primarily about entrepreneurship, which limits its reach to users who are specifically stuck about starting something. Insight density is moderate (narrative-heavy, with insights embedded in longer story arcs).

Episode curation guidance: Select episodes where the founding story explicitly includes a period of knowing they should start and not starting — the self-doubt, the delay, the moment they decided. Not all episodes have this. Avoid episodes focused primarily on growth strategy, marketing, or business mechanics.

*Status: Strong Candidate — valuable for Inaction Loop specifically*

---

**Modern Wisdom (Chris Williamson)**
- *Type:* Long-form interview podcast / YouTube
- *Host:* Chris Williamson
- *Preliminary score: 52 / 80 — Strong Candidate (second iteration)*

What makes it strong: Modern Wisdom targets the 25–40 growth-oriented demographic very directly, with episodes explicitly addressing career, purpose, relationships, and performance. Chris Williamson has made significant credibility gains in recent years. Guest quality is high (Cal Newport, James Clear, Ryan Holiday, Brené Brown all appear). The show's reach with the target demographic is significant.

Strongest states: Direction Collapse, Engagement Drought, Inaction Loop

Weaknesses: Host credibility is lower than the other shows in this tier — Chris Williamson is younger and less experienced than Tim Ferriss or Steven Bartlett. Insight density is variable (ranges from very good to quite surface depending on the episode). Some content tends toward the motivational and high-energy rather than the reflective and naming-the-experience quality Silhouette needs.

Episode curation guidance: Prioritize episodes with proven high-insight guests (Newport, Holiday, Clear) and where the topic explicitly addresses a stuck state. Avoid episodes focused on self-optimization tactics without personal narrative depth.

*Status: Strong Candidate — second iteration*

---

**Feel Better Live More (Dr. Rangan Chatterjee)**
- *Type:* Long-form interview podcast
- *Host:* Dr. Rangan Chatterjee
- *Preliminary score: 55 / 80 — Strong Candidate*

What makes it strong: Dr. Chatterjee is a UK-based GP who has built a large, trusted audience around health, purpose, and life quality. The show is explicitly about reconnecting with what matters — highly relevant to Engagement Drought and Direction Collapse. His medical credibility gives the show a different kind of authority than most hosts. High emotional resonance, thoughtful guest selection.

Strongest states: Engagement Drought, Direction Collapse

Weaknesses: The show's scope is broad (includes nutrition, sleep, physical health) and not all episodes are relevant to the stuck states. Some content skews toward behavioral health and lifestyle change rather than identity and purpose reframing.

Episode curation guidance: Select episodes explicitly focused on meaning, purpose, career transitions, and reconnecting with self. Avoid episodes primarily focused on diet, exercise, or sleep protocols.

*Status: Strong Candidate — particularly valuable for Engagement Drought content*

### 1.2 Solo Educational Podcasts

---

**Huberman Lab (Andrew Huberman)**
- *Type:* Solo educational podcast / YouTube
- *Host:* Andrew Huberman (Stanford neuroscientist)
- *Preliminary score: 66 / 80 — MVP Priority*

What makes it strong: Huberman Lab is the strongest available source for mechanism-based content — explaining *why* motivation fades, *why* procrastination happens, *why* the brain resists starting. This type of content is a distinct insight format that no interview show can replicate. Huberman's scientific credibility is exceptional and his communication skills are remarkable for an academic. Episodes on dopamine, procrastination, and goal-setting are directly relevant to Engagement Drought and Inaction Loop at the mechanism level.

Strongest states: Engagement Drought (dopamine, motivation neuroscience), Inaction Loop (procrastination, habit formation, executive function)

Weaknesses: Emotional resonance is lower than narrative interview sources — Huberman is teaching, not confessing. His personal narrative is present but secondary. Episodes run extremely long (2–4 hours) and require careful timestamp-based segmentation. Not all content is relevant — avoid episodes focused on physical protocols, sleep supplements, or performance optimization unrelated to stuck states.

Credibility note: Huberman content should be tagged as *mechanism* insight type, not *story* — the credibility comes from empirical research, not lived experience of the stuck states. This is a different and valuable type of credibility that needs to be reflected in corpus metadata.

Episode curation guidance: Focus on episodes explicitly about motivation, dopamine, procrastination, goal-setting, and mental health. Identify the specific segments within long episodes rather than ingesting full episodes.

*Status: MVP Priority — begin episode-level evaluation*

---

**Hidden Brain (Shankar Vedantam)**
- *Type:* Solo educational podcast (NPR)
- *Host:* Shankar Vedantam
- *Preliminary score: 60 / 80 — Strong Candidate*

What makes it strong: Hidden Brain translates social psychology and behavioral science into emotionally resonant storytelling — a rare combination. NPR format means excellent transcript quality and high production standards. The show addresses the mechanisms behind stuck states through research and narrative together. Very strong on explaining why comparison hurts us (Momentum Gap), why we don't do things we want to do (Inaction Loop), and what actually makes life feel meaningful (Direction Collapse). High temporal stability — social psychology research dates slowly.

Strongest states: Inaction Loop (procrastination, self-sabotage), Momentum Gap (comparison, social cognition), Direction Collapse (meaning and motivation research)

Weaknesses: Primarily research-based rather than personal narrative — specificity of experience is lower than interview sources. The "this is my personal story" quality is less present. Requires pairing with narrative sources for maximum impact.

Episode curation guidance: Select episodes that directly address the psychological mechanisms behind the MVP stuck states. The show's narrative format means insights are often embedded in longer story arcs — identify the specific research finding or reframe, not the full story arc.

*Status: Strong Candidate — particularly valuable for mechanism-type insights*

### 1.3 Author Interview Crossovers

The following are not standalone sources but recommended as curation targets across the interview podcast sources above. When evaluating episodes of approved shows, flag appearances by these speakers as high priority.

**Finding appearances:** Use [Listen Notes](https://www.listennotes.com/) (guest name search across podcast catalog), [Rephonic](https://rephonic.com/) (cross-show guest appearance tracking), or [Podchaser](https://www.podchaser.com/) (appearance history by person). Prioritize appearances on the highest-tier approved shows before expanding to secondary sources.

**David Goggins**

- *Preliminary score: 79 / 80 — MVP Priority*

His entire public body of work addresses the psychology of resistance, the lies we tell ourselves about what we're capable of, and the identity shift required to act despite every internal objection. Strongest states: Inaction Loop, Engagement Drought (the "push through when you don't feel like it" dimension)

Episode curation guidance: Goggins appears on many shows; prioritize his appearances on Tim Ferriss Show and similar high-quality hosts who know how to channel his content into specific moments rather than just intensity. His Joe Rogan appearances contain valuable material but are very long and require significant segmentation.

## Section 2.5: Resonance-to-Source Map

The state-to-source map above answers: which sources cover which stuck states? This map answers a different question: which sources serve which *kinds of users*?

Two sources can both be strong for Direction Collapse and still fail for different users — because one delivers reframe/intellectual content and the other delivers story/vulnerable content. Without this map, there is no principled basis for choosing between them based on who the user is. See `user_resonance_model.md` for the full definition of insight types and voice registers.

| Source | Primary Insight Type(s) | Voice Register |
|---|---|---|
| Tim Ferriss Show | Story, Reframe *(varies heavily by guest)* | Intellectual/Measured |
| Diary of a CEO | Story | Warm/Affirming |
| School of Greatness | Story, Permission | Warm/Affirming |
| Knowledge Project | Reframe | Intellectual/Measured |
| How I Built This | Story | Intellectual/Measured |
| Modern Wisdom | Reframe, Story *(varies by guest)* | Intellectual/Measured |
| Feel Better Live More | Story, Permission | Warm/Affirming |
| Huberman Lab | Mechanism | Expert/Scientific |
| Hidden Brain | Mechanism, Story | Expert/Scientific |
| David Goggins appearances | Story, Permission | Direct/Challenging |
| Matthew McConaughey appearances | Story | Vulnerable/Personal |
| Naval Ravikant appearances | Reframe | Intellectual/Measured |
| Ryan Holiday appearances | Reframe | Intellectual/Measured |
| Cal Newport appearances | Reframe | Intellectual/Measured |
| Mark Manson appearances | Reframe, Permission | Direct/Challenging |

**Note on multi-resonance sources:** Tim Ferriss Show and Modern Wisdom vary by guest — Naval on Tim Ferriss is reframe/intellectual; McConaughey on Tim Ferriss is story/vulnerable. Source-level resonance classifications above describe the show's default tone and host register.

**Resonance gap visible in this map:** Intellectual/Measured is overrepresented relative to Warm/Affirming and Vulnerable/Personal. This is acceptable for the MVP but should be monitored — users who need permission or story content in an affirming register are underserved by the current candidate list. Feel Better Live More, School of Greatness, and McConaughey appearances are the primary coverage; Diary of a CEO also addresses this register for some episodes.

## Section 3: Deprioritized and Excluded Sources

### Deprioritized — Not Excluded, But Not MVP

**Joe Rogan Experience**
*Reason for deprioritization:* Too variable, too long, too high a controversy risk for Silhouette's trust positioning. Some individual episodes (Naval Ravikant, David Goggins appearances) contain excellent content — but those guests are accessible through better hosts. The source itself does not meet the quality bar as a primary Silhouette source.
*Note:* Do not ingest from this show as a source. Guests who appear here can be found on approved shows with better episode structure.

**Jay Shetty (standalone content)**
*Reason for deprioritization:* Strong audience with the target demographic, but standalone content tends toward surface-level purpose advice ("here are 3 steps to find your meaning"). Higher-quality insight is available from his guest appearances on other shows. School of Greatness and Diary of a CEO have both featured him.
*Note:* Seek specific guest appearances on approved shows. Do not ingest his standalone content for MVP.

**Tony Robbins**
*Reason for deprioritization:* High-energy motivation content that doesn't align with the quiet, reflective state where a Silhouette user typically reaches for the product. Some concerns about business practices and public controversies that would not align well with Silhouette's trust positioning. Insight density is lower than it appears — theatrical technique substitutes for genuine reframe.
*Note:* Not recommended for MVP or second iteration.

### Excluded — Formally Rejected

**Gary Vaynerchuk / GaryVee**
*Reason for exclusion:* Content is primarily hustle culture — "work more, post more, document, grind." This does not address the stuck states in the User Problem Model in a useful way. The primary answer GaryVee offers to all stuck states is "work harder," which is not a reframe. Insight density is very low. Not a Silhouette source.

**Motivational compilation channels (YouTube)**
*Reason for exclusion:* Attribution is impossible — insights are aggregated from multiple sources without clear speaker identification, often over-dubbed with music, cropped and spliced without context. This directly contradicts Silhouette's core requirement of real, named, attributable human sources.

**Armchair Expert (Dax Shepard)**
*Reason for exclusion:* Entertainment-first format. Low insight density for the stuck states. Not a Silhouette source.

**Generic life coach / influencer content**
*Reason for exclusion:* Category-level exclusion. Creators whose primary credential is their own social media following, without substantive domain expertise or directly relevant lived experience, do not meet the Speaker Credibility minimum. This includes a large category of self-improvement content that looks like Silhouette's target space but lacks the credibility foundation to produce a genuine aha moment.

## Section 4: Next Steps for Source Validation

Before any source moves from candidate to approved, complete the following:

1. **Formal rubric scoring** — Score each MVP Priority candidate against the full rubric in `source_scoring_rubric.md`. Record the completed evaluation template for each.

2. **Episode-level review** — For each source scoring 65+ on the rubric, identify 3–5 specific episodes that appear to be highest-density for the MVP stuck states. Note episode titles, timestamps of key segments, and preliminary state tags.

3. **Transcript verification** — Confirm that clean text is obtainable for the identified episodes at manageable cost. Document the transcript source and quality for each.

4. **State coverage audit** — After formal scoring is complete, review the state-to-source fit map and identify any MVP states that lack sufficient coverage from the approved source set. Address gaps before ingestion begins.

5. **Approve the initial source list** — Document the final approved list with evaluation rationale before any ingestion begins.
