## Original vision behind Silhouette

I first got the idea for Silhouette when I was listening to the School of Greatness podcast during a season where I felt like I was in a rut.

At the time, life felt monotonous. My days all felt the same. My social life was not where I wanted it to be. I felt like I was just kind of going through the motions and didn’t have much excitement, clarity, or hope.

Then I listened to that podcast episode, and something changed. It gave me new insights and new ways of thinking that I had not been exposed to before. It did not magically solve everything, but it gave me a real sense of hope and possibility that I did not have before I listened. It made me feel like there were new ways to think, new ways to act, and maybe a way out of the rut I was in.

That moment is what inspired Silhouette.

## The core idea

The original idea for Silhouette was to build a platform that could take in input from users who feel like they are stuck in a rut, discouraged, overwhelmed, low on hope, or unsure how to move forward.

Then, instead of making them search for hours through random content, Silhouette would find the most relevant and helpful piece of advice, quote, clip, podcast segment, article, or new way of thinking for their exact problem.

The types of sources I imagined it drawing from were things like:
- School of Greatness
- Huberman Lab
- Dan Martell’s podcast
- other self-help, personal growth, business, mindset, and performance-oriented content in that same general world

The goal is not just to give users “content.”
The goal is to give them the right piece of hope, insight, or perspective at the right time, in an easily digestible way.

In other words:
Silhouette would save people from the pain of not knowing whether hope is even out there, and from having to spend hours searching through podcasts, YouTube videos, quotes, articles, or books just to maybe find one thing that speaks to them.

The product vision is:
- understand what kind of problem the person is struggling with
- identify the best resource or insight for that moment
- deliver it in a simple, digestible way
- help the user feel new hope, clarity, belief, or momentum

## What feels important to me about the idea

The most important part of this idea is not “AI” for the sake of AI.
It is the idea of helping people quickly find the exact insight, perspective, or encouragement they need when they are in a hard season.

I really believe one meaningful insight or one clip or one line from the right person can change someone’s trajectory, or at least give them the emotional spark they need to start moving again.

That is what I want Silhouette to eventually capture.
